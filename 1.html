Biography

Xuepei Wang is an lecturer in the School of Integrated Circuit Science and Engineering at Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications (NJUPT). His research centers on energy-efficient computing hardware beyond the Moore era, including hafnium-based ferroelectric devices, in-memory/near-memory computing accelerators, neuromorphic chips, and Hardware security encryption (PUF and TENG). He has published over 20 papers in top-tier venues (IEEE EDL, IEEE TED, APL, Nano Energy, ACS AMI, IEEE EDTM etc.), with more than 1,000 citations (Google Scholar) and an h-index of 16.

Research Interests

  • Ferroelectric Memory(FeRAM, FeFET, and FTJ)
  • Computing-In-Memory & Near-Memory-Computing
  • Neuromorphic Hardware & Spiking Neural Networks
  • Hardware Security Encryption (PUF and TRNG)
